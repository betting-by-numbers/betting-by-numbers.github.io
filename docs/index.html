A Sports Betting Story

Revenue from legalized sports betting has surged in recent years, driven by a 2018 Supreme Court ruling that overturned the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA). The decision allowed individual states to legalize and regulate sports wagering, transforming what was once a largely prohibited activity into a booming industry.

As of March 2025, sports betting is legal in 38 states and the District of Columbia, with 30 of these jurisdictions also permitting online wagering. In 2023, Americans legally wagered nearly $14 billion on sports, according to the American Gaming Association.

This article explores the demographics of U.S. sports bettors and the varied outcomes of sports betting.

Background on Bettors

Nearly four in ten Americans bet on sporting events and one in five have an online account with a sports betting service such as DraftKings, Caesars, or FanDuel. Those with online accounts tend to be younger and less educated and are more likely to be male and from the south.

Betting Outcomes

Most sports bettors win modest amounts when they land their bets. Nearly sixty percent of survey respondents said their biggest single-day win on an online sportsbook was under $200. One Bettor's Story

The rapid growth of sports betting has raised concerns about gambling addiction. Research published in JAMA Internal Medicine indicates that sports gambling wagers surged from $4.9 billion in 2017 to $121 billion in 2023, leading to a 23% rise in online searches for gambling addiction help.
